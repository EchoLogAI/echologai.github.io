About EchoLog

Building accessible, voice-driven and developer-friendly tools.

Our Mission

EchoLog exists to make computers more accessible, comfortable, and intuitive to use—especially for people with motor disabilities, visual impairments, repetitive strain injuries (RSI), or anyone who benefits from hands-free computing. We believe that accessibility should be built into everyday tools, not added as an afterthought.

What We’re Building

  • EchoLog AI Voice Assistant (Chrome extension): A voice-first assistant that helps you navigate your computer and the web with natural language commands, reducing the need for constant mouse and keyboard use.
  • JSON Formatter: A free browser-based utility at https://jsonformatter.echologai.com that lets developers quickly format, validate, and inspect JSON data directly in the browser.
  • Upcoming Protobuf Tool: We are working on a protobuf compiler–related tool to simplify working with protocol buffers, especially for developers who want fast feedback and clear error handling.

Together, these tools are part of a growing ecosystem focused on accessibility and developer productivity.

Our Principles

  • Accessibility First: We design with users who rely on assistive technologies at the center of the experience.
  • Privacy by Design: We minimize data collection and keep as much computation as possible on the user’s device. Details on how we handle data are available in our Privacy Policy.
  • Developer-Friendly: Our utilities are built to be simple, fast, and practical for everyday workflows.

Who We Are

EchoLog is built by a small, focused team that cares deeply about accessibility and tooling. We combine experience across software engineering, AI, and user experience design to create products that feel both powerful and approachable.
